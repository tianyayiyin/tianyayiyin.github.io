打开帖子 
The least annoying follow-ups are usually the most predictable ones. I would set a cadence before the invoice is late:

- 3 days before due: confirm they received it and ask if it is scheduled
- due date: short reminder, no emotion
- 7 days overdue: ask for a specific payment date
- 14 days overdue: mention that new work pauses if it is not brought current

The important part is tracking the last reminder and the next promised payment date. Most owners do not need harsher wording first; they need a system that prevents invoices from living in memory.

中文评论：换收款账户

突然换收款账户一定要当成高风险，不管对方邮件写得多像真的。最好用原来备案电话或合同联系人确认，不要用邮件里新给的电话。很多付款损失不是会计问题，是付款前少了这一步核验。

Reddit：重复付款

The best prevention is a tiny pre-payment duplicate check before the money moves:

- same invoice number already seen
- same vendor + amount in the last 30-60 days
- invoice sent to both a personal inbox and billing inbox
- payment status already marked paid
- credit note or refund already pending

It is boring, but boring is exactly what you want here. Duplicate payments usually come from messy handoff, not complicated accounting.
